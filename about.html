About GIN

GIN stands for the Global Issues Network. GIN is a worldwide 'student-led local, regional, global impact group.' GIN started at the International School of Luxembourg in 2003 when educators recognized youth needed a platform to act on the biggest challenges facing our world. GIN is active at ASIJ!

GIN at ASIJ empowers middle school students to become changemakers through project-based learning aligned to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Students collaborate to design and lead initiatives that create meaningful impact in our school and local community.

We value student voice, inclusion, and sustainability. Whether you're passionate about environmental action, equity, or innovation, there's a place for you in GIN.

Our Partnerships

Sneaker Kids Tokyo

Sneaker Kids Tokyo is a Japan-based organization focused on donating sneakers to underprivileged communities and orphanages in Cebu, Philippines. The founder first went to Cebu and saw many kids playing basketball‚Äîhis favorite sport‚Äîbarefoot on uneven pavement. This inspired him to take action.

Their Journey: He first delivered shoes to 15 orphans in 2014, gathering shoes from his sister's basketball team. However, they were too big for the young children. He promised to return in 3 months with proper sneakers. They started collecting unwanted sneakers through social media and word of mouth, personally delivering them to the Philippines.

Impact: Over 10 years, with 50+ volunteers, they've donated over 13,000 pairs of shoes and traveled to the Philippines over 25 times. In August, they went to Cebu and donated over 600 pairs of shoes, visiting 8 orphanages in Bogo, Sogod, and Cebu city.

Why It Matters: There are over 1.8 million orphans in the Philippines. For many children, receiving donated sneakers is their first experience of choosing something for themselves. Some children don't wear their received sneakers every day because they are so precious to them. The goal is to ensure every child can wear sneakers daily.

GIN's Collaboration: On October 29, 2024, GIN invited Sneaker Kids Tokyo to ASIJ to learn more about changemaking and explore collaboration opportunities. We finalized plans for the 2025 sneaker drive with a goal of collecting many more sneakers than the previous year.
