What is GIN?

GIN stands for the Global Issues Network — a worldwide student-led local, regional, and global impact movement. GIN began at the International School of Luxembourg in 2003 when educators recognized youth needed a platform to act on the biggest challenges facing our world. GIN is active at ASIJ, empowering student changemakers to learn, lead, and act through real projects.

Sustainability Tip of the Week

The Hidden Cost of Fast Fashion

  • The fashion industry produces about 10% of global carbon emissions — more than international flights and shipping combined.
  • It can take roughly 2,700 liters of water to produce a cotton t-shirt — about what one person drinks in 3 years.
  • GIN is hosting a clothing drive until November 7th. Remember: Eco fashion is a MUSTang passion!

Featured Activities

Winter Fest: Sustainable Giving & Animal Adoption

Join GIN at Winter Fest, ASIJ's largest annual event with over 3,000 community members! Last year marked the 50th anniversary of Winterfest.

  • Animal adoption event with ARK (Animal Refugee Kansai) - meet dogs and hamsters looking for homes!
  • Learn about sustainable giving: low-waste gift ideas and responsible holiday shopping.
  • Support donation drives that give a second life to clothing and supplies.

Spirit Day Eco Challenges

Show your Mustang spirit while taking on sustainability challenges! Spirit Day strengthens our community with live concerts, international food booths, and impact booths.

  • FUN RECYCLING booth: Learn proper sorting for recycling, compost, and trash through interactive games.
  • Clothing Drive & Swap: "Eco Fashion is a MUSTANG PASSION" - collect and swap 2nd hand clothing.
  • Join our Spirit Day photo wall to celebrate sustainable choices.

Year-Round Impact Projects

Beyond big events, GIN runs student-led projects all year long:

  • Sneaker Drives: Partnership with Sneaker Kids Tokyo - collected 379 pairs in 2025 for orphanages in Cebu, Philippines.
  • Bake Sales: Supporting refugees in the Middle East with Help4Refugees - raised ¥200,000+ in 2 years.
  • Earth Day: Recycled-caps mural and sustainability quiz games.
  • Book Drives: Partnering with Papaya Academy to support their library.
  • Design your own project with mentor support - we'll help you make it happen!
